# Feasibility sensitivity matrix — where does investor NPV turn positive?

Generated by `python -m analysis.sensitivity_matrix`. Every combination run through the live engine; PASS = investor (owner) NPV > 0.

## Headline

- **3 of 288** commercial-factorial combinations pass. They cluster where the favourable levers stack up.
- Passing combinations by case: Ealing Phase 1 3.
- Every pass needs **heat-pump parity** billing; almost all need **avoided-capital capture** on top of GHNF. Gas-parity billing never passes.
- **Contractor and operator NPV are positive across essentially every scheme** — they are paid out of CAPEX/OPEX first; only the owner holds the residual (see SM2).
- The physical levers (heating dT, cooling dT, 2- vs 4-pipe, climate, electricity price) move owner NPV far less than the commercial levers — see SM3. For an EfW-baseload stack, flow temperature barely moves NPV (smaller pipes offset lower COP).
