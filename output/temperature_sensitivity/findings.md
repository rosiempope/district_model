## Bounds — the standards, not taste

- Flow **55–70°C**: CP1 2020 sets 70°C as the maximum for new schemes and permits down to 55°C.
- Return: CP1 2020 best practice is a VWART below **33°C**.
- CIBSE's heat-pump-led target is **60/30**.
- Delivered floor: **55°C** with instantaneous HIUs (50°C outlet, HSE 'low risk', +5K approach) or **65°C** with stored cylinders (60°C stored per HSG274, +5K coil).

and note what widening ΔT does to it.

## Read-out

- Today's design (70/40, instantaneous HIU): NPV £-32.47m, mean ASHP COP 2.53, delivered 68.8°C (**+13.8K** against its floor — unused margin).
- Best compliant, instantaneous HIU: **60/25**, NPV £-30.73m, COP 2.90 (**+14.5%** vs 70/40), NPV delta **£+1.74m**.
- Best compliant with **stored cylinders**: 70/25, NPV £-31.91m. The cylinder's 65°C floor forecloses the low-temperature options entirely — the DHW system choice, not the network, is what gates the COP gain.
